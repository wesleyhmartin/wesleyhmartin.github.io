What I Like and Don't Like About the DA2 Player

I received my DA2 player a few weeks ago, and I like it overall. Let me address its pros and cons.

The DA2 player is a bit wider than the DS1/DA1, which I will refer to from now on as the "old" players. It appears to have many more buttons at first glance, but actually only has a few more. It's also slightly lighter than the old ones.

What I Like About It

Bluetooth

Finally, it has Bluetooth! You can connect earbuds, headphones, glasses, hearing aids, speakers, and more. No support for Bluetooth keyboards or Braille displays, though.

BARD Support

It allows downloading books and magazines from BARD, plus adding them to your wish list. Super handy.

Date and Time

I've pined for this for years! It's weird you have to set it manually even though it's online, but you only do it once, so no biggie.

USB-C Charging

No more power cord in the back—hallelujah! And it connects directly to your PC. No keyboard or audio device support here, though.

32GB Internal Storage

A solid amount for NLS books, which are relatively small in size.

No Specific Audio Folder Anymore

Place audio files anywhere. Each folder is treated as a separate book labeled “Other Books.” Could be a pro or a con.

Includes Author Names on the Bookshelf

A feature I didn’t know I wanted until I had it. Press Info for more details like book number and description.

Better Speaker

Vastly improved audio compared to the old players. Maybe it's just me, but I love it.

The Jingles

Startup, shutdown, etc. all have cute little jingles. Chef’s kiss.

Much More Responsive Overall

No more sluggishness. Press a key, get a result. The volume control really showcases this.

Play/Stop Bookshelf

There's now a dedicated Bookshelf button, but you can still press and hold Play/Stop like the good ol’ days.

Support for NTFS

Supports the NTFS file system, which means larger-capacity flash drives. Win!

Things to Improve

This is a free product, so perfection isn’t expected. I appreciate the hard work, and some issues may be fixed in updates.

Text-to-Speech

This one’s a toss-up. It’s less natural-sounding, and I’ll miss “Player on.” RIP old-school charm.

Wi-Fi

It’s... there. But it’s slow and doesn’t always find networks well. Needs improvement.

Speed Adjustment Skipping

Adjusting speed causes the audio to skip. With TTS, it might even jump to the next section. Not ideal.

Voices

The A Capella voices aren’t my favorite—neither natural-sounding nor responsive. Limited options for now.

Braille Markings Are Confusing

Some markings are unclear at first, and button positions vary. But once learned, it's manageable.

Startup Time

It takes way longer to start and refresh the bookshelf compared to the old model. Patience is a virtue.

Overall, a great improvement. Thanks to the Library for their consistently excellent service!
