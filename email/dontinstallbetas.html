I'm breaking this out because it's important and it applies to each and every major NVDA release.  When I say major I mean when the year number changes.  All dot level releases within a single year numbered release are minor releases (even when the changes they make are significant).

 

DO NOT RUSH TO UPDATE.  It's that simple, when a major release beta comes out all the way through the initial release.  Until and unless you are certain that any add-ons that you absolutely must have exist in either a compatible, tested version or someone who is not as reliant upon those add-ons as you are has confirmed that doing a override for the incompatible version still allows it to function, just don't update NVDA.

 

As an alternative, you could elect to create a portable version of your existing NVDA, with user settings/configuration included during the creation of that portable, and update.  But you'd still likely end up using your portable copy because there are add-ons you just can't live without.

 

My core advice here is make certain that you do not lose access to the version of NVDA and all add-ons that you rely upon by being an early adopter.  With a little care, this can be avoided.

 

I am going to quote something that Joseph Lee just posted, where over time the NVDA release number may change, and the number of add-ons in the Add-On store may change, but the broad principles do not change:

----

A few things to note about add-ons:

  • There are 252 add-ons registered on NV Access add-on store, and 21 are marked compatible with NVDA 2025.1 but these add-ons are dev and beta releases. You won't see stable add-on channel updates until 2025.1 release candidate is released in a few weeks.
  • Some speech synthesizer add-ons are discontinued, and for others, they will require modifications. Depending on modifications made, synthesizer add-ons will not work properly on earlier NVDA releases (this is due to changes to audio device setting).
  • NVDA Remote Support add-on is now part of NVDA.
  • NVDA now supports automatic add-on updates. Therefore, Add-on Updater is officially discontinued (I will release a farewell release soon).
  • As always, check out what's new document for more information.
